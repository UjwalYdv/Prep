# 💼 Interview Experience – Goldman Sachs Summer Analyst Intern

**Type:** On-Campus Opportunity  
**Role:** Summer Analyst Intern  
**Total Rounds:** 3 (Technical Interviews)

---

## 🌳 Round 1 – Technical Interview

The first round focused on **core Computer Science concepts** and **resume-based discussions**.

- Questions on **Tree definitions** and **Level Order Traversal** → [Level Order Traversal | LeetCode #102](https://leetcode.com/problems/binary-tree-level-order-traversal/)
- In-depth questions from the **resume**, including **Android Development**-related topics
- Discussion on **Object-Oriented Programming (OOPs)** — concepts like inheritance, abstraction, and encapsulation

🧠 **Tip:** Be thorough with all the concepts and technologies mentioned in your resume — they form the majority of this round.

✅ **Outcome:** Shortlisted for next round

---

## 🕸️ Round 2 – Graphs, OOPs, and Resume Deep Dive

This was a **notepad-based technical round**, where the candidate was asked to **solve graph problems on paper** and **explain the approach clearly**.

- **Graph Questions:**
  - Common graph traversal or path-finding questions → [Graph Traversal (BFS/DFS) | GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/breadth-first-search-or-bfs-for-a-graph/)
  - Concepts related to **graph representation, adjacency list, and cycle detection**
- Further **OOPs questions** (real-world applications and examples)
- **Detailed resume discussion**, focusing on **project implementation details and learning outcomes**

✅ **Outcome:** Shortlisted for the final round

---

## 💻 Round 3 – OA Question Discussion

In the final round, the interviewer revisited **Online Assessment (OA) questions**.  
The candidate was asked to **re-solve and explain** one of the OA coding problems from earlier rounds.  
However, due to insufficient preparation, the solution couldn’t be completed optimally.

❌ **Outcome:** Rejected after this round

---

## 🌟 Overall Experience

The overall experience was **decent and professional**.  
Interviewers were **polite and focused on depth of understanding** rather than just surface-level answers.  
Each round progressively evaluated **problem-solving ability, conceptual knowledge, and project depth**.  
Even though the candidate wasn’t selected, the process offered valuable insights into **how Goldman Sachs assesses both technical and personal readiness**.

---

## 💡 Advice for Others

> ⚡ **Prepare well for your Online Assessment (OA) questions, they often come back in final interviews.**

Revise the **DSA questions you solved during OA**, brush up on **OOPs**, and be confident about your **project explanations**.  
A well-prepared and structured approach helps you handle both technical and follow-up questions effectively.
