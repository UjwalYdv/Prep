# 💼 Interview Experience – Goldman Sachs Summer Analyst Intern

**Type:** On-Campus Opportunity  
**Role:** Summer Analyst Intern  
**Total Rounds:** 4 (Online Assessment + 3 Technical Interviews)

---

## 💻 Round 1 – Online Assessment

The first round was an **online assessment** that included a **mix of MCQs and coding questions**.

- There were **3 coding problems**:
  - **AVL Tree Implementation** → [Implement AVL Tree | GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/avl-tree-set-1-insertion/)
  - **Simple Math Problem (Factor Calculation)** → [Count Divisors of a Number | GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/dsa/total-number-divisors-given-number/)
- The **MCQs** were mainly **puzzle-type logical questions**
- Additionally, there was **one behavioral question** to test situational thinking

🧠 **Tip:** Practice **data structure implementation**, **math-based logic**, and **basic reasoning puzzles** before the test.

✅ **Outcome:** Shortlisted for Technical Interviews

---

## ⚙️ Round 2 – Offline Technical Interview

This round started with a **short introduction** followed by a discussion on the **candidate’s project** — focusing on what it does and how it was built.  
Then, the interviewer gave a **BFS-based coding problem**, similar to the **Rotten Oranges** question.

- **Rotten Oranges Problem** → [Rotting Oranges | LeetCode #994](https://leetcode.com/problems/rotting-oranges/)

The candidate solved it quickly, showing clarity in approach and confidence.

✅ **Outcome:** Moved to next round

---

## ⚙️ Round 3 – Deep Technical + Resume Round

This round was a **detailed deep dive** into the **resume and projects**.  
The interviewer asked several **OOPs-based questions**, such as:

- What is an **abstract class**?
- Explain **runtime polymorphism**.
- What is **method overloading**?

Then came two **DSA problems**:

- **Sudoku Solver** → [Sudoku Solver | LeetCode #37](https://leetcode.com/problems/sudoku-solver/)
- **Valid Parentheses** → [Valid Parentheses | LeetCode #20](https://leetcode.com/problems/valid-parentheses/)

The candidate successfully solved both problems.  
The round ended with a **5-minute discussion**, where the candidate asked questions to the interviewer and had a friendly closing chat.

✅ **Outcome:** Shortlisted for Final Round

---

## 🧩 Round 4 – Puzzle + Discussion Round

The final round was more **analytical and conversational**.

- The interviewer asked **two puzzle questions** — the first was solved easily, and the second required hints.
- The round concluded with a **discussion about the impact of AI** in the current tech world, lasting about **5 minutes**.

This round was short and focused on **problem-solving ability and perspective**.

✅ **Outcome:** Selected for Goldman Sachs Summer Analyst Intern

---

## 🌟 Overall Experience

The overall experience was **nice and smooth**.  
The interviewers were **polite, engaging, and professional** throughout the process.  
They focused on understanding the **candidate’s thought process**, not just the final answers.  
The progression of rounds felt **logical and fair**, with a good balance between **technical and conceptual discussions**.

---

## 💡 Advice for Others

> ⚡ **Prepare DSA and make sure you know everything that you have written in your resume.**

Goldman Sachs focuses on **concept clarity, confidence, and in-depth understanding** of your projects.  
Be honest about your work, and revise all **core computer science concepts** thoroughly.
