# 💼 Interview Experience – Optum TDP Intern

**Type:** On-Campus Opportunity  
**Role:** Technology Development Program (Intern)  
**Total Rounds:** 2 (HR + Technical)

---

## 🗣️ Round 1 – HR Interview

After the **OA shortlisting results** were announced, the next day we had the HR round.

This was a **casual conversation round** focused on personality and communication.  
I was first asked to **introduce myself**, and then a few common questions followed, such as:

- “What are your interests?”
- “Why Optum?”
- “How do you deal with failure?”

The interviewer had a list of questions prepared and referred to them during the interview.  
There were **no technical questions**, and the discussion lasted around **10–12 minutes**.

💡 **Tip:** Keep your answers natural and confident. Talk about real experiences -> it shows emotional intelligence and self-awareness.

📘 Helpful Reads:

- [HR Interview Questions – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/hr/hr-interview-questions/)
- [Behavioral Interview Questions – Indeed](https://www.indeed.com/career-advice/interviewing/behavioral-interview-questions)

✅ **Outcome:** Cleared HR Round

---

## ⚙️ Round 2 – Technical Interview

The **technical round** was scheduled **two days later**.  
It started with a short self-introduction, followed by a detailed discussion on my **resume** and **interests**.

Since I had mentioned **DSA and problem-solving**, the interviewer asked a few conceptual questions:

- “Name some sorting algorithms.”
- “Can you explain hash sort and how it works?”

📘 To brush up:

- [Sorting Algorithms – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/sorting-algorithms/)
- [Hashing and Hash Functions – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/hashing-data-structure/)

After that, we moved on to my **projects**, which were related to **Machine Learning**.  
Since the interviewer was from a **data science background**, they asked:

- “What’s the difference between supervised and unsupervised learning?”
- “What are training and testing datasets?”
- “Which libraries did you use?”
- “Explain a few Python-specific concepts you applied.”

We ended the interview with a discussion on the **recent developments in AI** and how **Optum** is integrating these technologies into healthcare solutions.  
It felt more like a **conversation than an evaluation**, which made the experience much smoother.

📘 Recommended Reading:

- [Machine Learning Basics – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/machine-learning/machine-learning/)
- [Supervised vs Unsupervised Learning – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/difference-between-supervised-and-unsupervised-learning/)
- [Important Python Libraries for ML – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/blogs/python-libraries-to-know/)

✅ **Outcome:** Selected for Optum TDP Intern

---

## 🌟 Overall Experience

The experience was **truly inspiring**.  
I learned a lot from refining my **soft skills** to revising all my technical concepts within just a couple of nights.  
Each conversation gave me insight into how interviews are as much about your **attitude and curiosity** as they are about your technical skills.

The interviewer was approachable and engaging, especially during the discussion about AI and how Optum is evolving in the healthcare technology space.

---

## 💡 Advice for Others

💪 **Never give up.**  
It might sound cliché, but it’s one of the truest things about the placement journey.  
Rejections happen — a lot — but every interview makes you better. Keep showing up.

🗣️ **Work on your soft skills and communication.**  
They are _game changers_. No matter how good your technical knowledge is, the way you express it defines how interviewers perceive your potential.

🔍 **Research the company.**  
Understand Optum’s mission, its connection with **UnitedHealth Group**, and the kind of work they do in **AI, analytics, and healthcare innovation**.  
It helps you hold meaningful conversations that leave a lasting impression.
