# 💼 Interview Experience – Optum TDP

**Type:** On-Campus Opportunity  
**Role:** Technology Development Program (TDP)  
**Total Rounds:** 2 (HR + Technical)

---

## 🗣️ Round 1 – HR Interview (Duration: 10 minutes)

The first round was an **HR interview** that mainly focused on my **personality, communication skills**, and **project experiences**.

Some of the key questions included:

- What challenges did you face in your projects, and how did you overcome them?
- How did you manage deadlines during your project work?
- How was your online assessment experience?
- Why do you want to join Optum?

🧠 To prepare for such questions, refer to:

- [HR Interview Questions – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/hr/hr-interview-questions/)
- [Top 20 HR Questions with Sample Answers – PrepInsta](https://prepinsta.com/interview-preparation/hr-interview-questions/)

The interviewer was friendly and conversational, focusing on my attitude and clarity of thought.  
I was informed that I had **cleared this round** and was **shortlisted for the Technical Round**.

✅ **Outcome:** Cleared HR Round

---

## ⚙️ Round 2 – Technical Interview (Duration: 1 hour 10 minutes)

The second round was a **detailed technical discussion** that revolved around my **projects**, **core skills**, and **computer science fundamentals**.

The interview began with a short self-introduction, followed by a deep dive into my **project work**.  
Some of the questions asked were:

- What technical skills do you have, and how have you improved them?
- How do you create a React project from scratch?  
  🔗 [React Project Setup – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/reactjs/reactjs-environment-setup/)
- Which build tool or project manager do you prefer (Vite, npm, or create-react-app)?  
  🔗 [Vite vs CRA vs Webpack](https://www.axelerant.com/blog/vite-vs-webpack-the-best-react-bundler)
- What are **props** in React, and can you share real-world use cases for them?  
  🔗 [React Props Explained – React.dev](https://react.dev/learn/passing-props-to-a-component)
- How do you ensure **security** while making backend API calls?  
  🔗 [Securing API Calls in React](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/65063730/secure-api-call-in-react-js)
- Explain **JWT (JSON Web Token)** and its use in authentication.  
  🔗 [JWT Authentication Explained – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/web-tech/json-web-token-jwt/)
- How can state be managed in a **URL** instead of a database?  
  🔗 [URL State Management in React](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/reactjs/state-management-in-react-hooks-context-api-and-redux/)
- What do you know about **AI/ML concepts** as a beginner?  
  🔗 [Machine Learning Basics – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/machine-learning/machine-learning/)
- What challenges did you face during your projects, and how did you resolve them?
- What was the **main purpose or outcome** of your project?

After discussing projects, the interviewer moved to **core CS concepts** such as:

- **OOPs Concepts:** Explain the four pillars of OOPs with examples.  
  🔗 [OOPs Concepts in Java – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/object-oriented-programming-oops-concept-in-java/)
- **Graph Theory:** What is a bipartite graph? (I was also asked to code a related problem.)  
  🔗 [Check if Graph is Bipartite – LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problems/is-graph-bipartite/)
- **Algorithms:** Write a sorting algorithm with **O(n)** time complexity.  
  🔗 [Counting Sort – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/counting-sort/)
- Discussion on **Kadane’s Algorithm** (no coding required).  
  🔗 [Kadane’s Algorithm – Maximum Subarray Sum – LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problems/maximum-subarray/)

The interviewer was highly engaging and appreciated clear explanations with structured reasoning.

✅ **Outcome:** Selected for Optum TDP

---

## 🌟 Overall Experience

The **Optum TDP interview process** was extremely well-structured and smooth.  
Both rounds were **interactive**, and the interviewers focused on **understanding my thought process** rather than just testing memorized answers.

The technical round tested both **depth of understanding** and **clarity of explanation**, especially in project-related discussions and conceptual reasoning.  
Overall, it was a **great learning experience** that strengthened my confidence in both technical and soft skills.

---

## 💡 Advice for Others

- **Know your projects inside out.** Expect deep, scenario-based questions about your tech stack, decisions, and challenges.
- Brush up on **React fundamentals** and be ready for conceptual questions like props, state, and lifecycle methods.
- Revise **core CS subjects** — especially **OOPs, Graphs, and Sorting Algorithms**.
- Don’t rush your answers — explain your thought process clearly.
- For HR preparation, refer to: [HR Interview Questions – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/hr/hr-interview-questions/).
