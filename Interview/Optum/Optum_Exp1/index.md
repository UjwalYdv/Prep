# 💼 Interview Experience – Optum TDP

**Type:** On-Campus Opportunity  
**Role:** Technology Development Program (TDP)  
**Total Rounds:** 3 (Online Assessment + HR + Technical)

---

## 🧠 Round 1 – Online Assessment

The first round was quite different from the usual coding tests.  
We had to **choose one tech stack**, and based on that, a **mini-project with four tasks** was assigned.

The interesting part was that the platform provided **AI assistance** to help complete the tasks.  
It was a **unique and interactive online assessment** that tested both **problem-solving and adaptability** rather than pure coding.

I successfully completed **3 out of 4 tasks**, which helped me qualify for the next round.

✅ **Outcome:** Shortlisted for HR Round

---

## 🗣️ Round 2 – HR Interview

This round started with a brief **introduction** and a few **general HR questions**.  
The interviewer also asked me to describe my **projects**, my role in them, and what I had learned from those experiences.

The conversation was light and friendly — mostly focused on my **resume** and **communication skills**.  
This round lasted around **15 minutes**.

✅ **Outcome:** Cleared HR Round

---

## ⚙️ Round 3 – Technical Interview

The final round was a **technical discussion** centered around my **projects**.  
The interviewer went in-depth into my **tech stack choices**, asking:

- Why I used a specific technology
- What other options were available
- How I would compare them in terms of performance, scalability, and efficiency

They didn’t ask any major **DSA questions**, but instead focused on my **understanding of technologies** and how I applied them in real-world projects.

✅ **Outcome:** Cleared Technical Round

---

## 🌟 Overall Experience

It was a **great learning experience** and one of the most **well-organized on-campus drives** I attended.  
The rounds were smooth, and the interviewers were friendly and professional throughout the process.

---

## 💡 Advice for Others

- **Know your projects thoroughly.** Anything you mention in your resume is fair game for questions.
- Avoid memorized HR answers — instead, prepare thoughtful, genuine responses.
- Revise key concepts of your chosen **tech stack** and be ready to justify your choices.
- Practice HR questions here: [HR Interview Questions – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/hr/hr-interview-questions/)
