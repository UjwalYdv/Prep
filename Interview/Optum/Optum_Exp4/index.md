# 💼 Interview Experience – Optum TDP Intern

**Type:** On-Campus Opportunity  
**Role:** Technology Development Program (Intern)  
**Total Rounds:** 3 (Online Assessment + HR + Technical)

---

## 🧮 Round 1 – Online Assessment

The first round was an **MCQ-based online test** covering multiple domains of **Computer Science fundamentals**, including:

- Data Structures and Algorithms
- OOPs and DBMS
- Computer Networks
- Basic Aptitude and Logical Reasoning

This round primarily tested conceptual understanding and problem-solving accuracy.  
Candidates who cleared this round were shortlisted for the **HR round**, conducted later the same day.

✅ **Outcome:** Cleared Online Assessment

---

## 🗣️ Round 2 – HR Interview (Duration: 10–12 minutes)

The HR round began with a **self-introduction**, followed by a friendly discussion about my **projects**.  
While introducing myself, I mentioned my technical projects, which led to deeper questions about:

- The objective of my project
- The tools and technologies used
- My role and contributions in the team

The round mainly assessed **communication**, **clarity**, and **confidence**.  
It was conversational and comfortable, helping me build rapport with the interviewer.

✅ **Outcome:** Cleared HR Round

---

## ⚙️ Round 3 – Technical Interview

The final round was conducted **four days later** and focused on **problem-solving and basic DSA**.  
I was asked to solve two coding problems — one based on **strings** and another on **arrays**, both of **easy to medium difficulty**.

📘 **Suggested Practice Problems:**

- [Reverse Words in a String – LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problems/reverse-words-in-a-string/)
- [Move Zeroes – LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problems/move-zeroes/)
- [Two Sum – LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problems/two-sum/)

After the coding section, the interviewer asked some **behavioral questions** such as:

- “Why do you want to join Optum?”
- “What are your strengths and weaknesses?”
- “Do you have any questions for me?”

The interview concluded with a short Q&A where I was encouraged to ask questions back, which made the session feel two-way and engaging.

✅ **Outcome:** Selected for Optum TDP Intern

---

## 🌟 Overall Experience

The overall process was **smooth, professional, and candidate-friendly**.  
Each round was structured to evaluate both **technical ability** and **communication skills**.  
Interviewers were patient and approachable, which made the experience far less stressful.  
It felt more like a conversation about my journey than a formal interrogation — which helped bring out my genuine personality and enthusiasm for the role.

---

## 💡 Advice for Others

✨ **Know your resume thoroughly.**

Be confident about every project and technology you mention — if it’s there, it’s fair game.

🗣️ **Stay interactive during the interview.**  
It’s not only about answering correctly but also about how well you connect and communicate with the interviewer.

😊 **Don’t forget to smile!**  
Your positive attitude can make a big difference.

❓ **Always ask questions at the end.**  
Do your research on the company’s **recent projects or initiatives**, and ask thoughtful questions that show genuine curiosity — it leaves a lasting impression.
