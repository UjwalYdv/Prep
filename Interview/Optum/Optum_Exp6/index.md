# 💼 Interview Experience – Optum TDP Intern

**Type:** On-Campus Opportunity  
**Role:** Technology Development Program (Intern)  
**Total Rounds:** 2 (HR + Technical)

---

## 🗣️ Round 1 – HR Interview

The first round was a **standard HR discussion** focused on behavioral and psychological understanding.  
The interviewer mainly tried to gauge **how I think, react, and communicate** in general situations.

Some of the questions asked included:

- “What skills did you learn in college that can help you in the corporate world (other than technical skills)?”
- “How do you usually handle disagreements in a team?”
- “What motivates you to do your best work?”

The tone of the round was friendly and conversational, with an emphasis on **personality, mindset, and interpersonal skills** rather than technical depth.

- [Behavioral Interview Questions – Indeed](https://www.indeed.com/career-advice/interviewing/behavioral-interview-questions)

✅ **Outcome:** Cleared HR Round

## ⚙️ Round 2 – Technical Interview (Duration: ~1 hour 15 minutes)

This round turned out to be **a mix of HR and technical discussions**.  
The interviewer was an **Associate Director**, and surprisingly, the first **one hour** felt more like a deep personality and mindset conversation.

He asked questions such as:

- “What philosophy do you believe in your life?”
- “How do you handle failure or criticism?”
- “What defines success for you?”

The discussion was thought-provoking and tested my **emotional intelligence**, **consistency**, and **self-awareness** rather than just technical skill.

In the last part of the interview, I was asked:

- One **DSA problem**, which was based on arrays (medium-level).  
  📘 Practice similar problems here:
  - [Two Sum – LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problems/two-sum/)
  - [Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock – LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problems/best-time-to-buy-and-sell-stock/)

Finally, the interviewer asked me about my **Machine Learning project**, where I had **replicated a neural network library** — quite an advanced topic.  
We discussed the project architecture, activation functions, and what challenges I faced during implementation.

If you’re working on similar topics, you can refer to:

- [Neural Networks from Scratch – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/deep-learning/neural-networks-a-beginners-guide/)
- [Building a Simple Neural Network in Python](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/numpy/implementation-of-neural-network-from-scratch-using-numpy/)

✅ **Outcome:** Selected for Optum TDP Intern

## 🌟 Overall Experience

The overall experience was **very good and insightful**.  
Unlike typical interviews, this one tested **depth of thinking, composure, and reasoning** as much as technical skill.  
The interviewer’s unique style pushed me to think critically about my beliefs and approach, making it one of the most memorable interviews I’ve had.

---

## 💡 Advice for Others

✨ **Be yourself — not rehearsed.**  
Many interviews at this level are designed to test **your authenticity**, not your ability to give perfect answers.

🧩 **Prepare your resume like a story.**  
Be ready to talk about every project or achievement with passion and clarity, especially if it’s something complex like ML or AI.

💬 **Don’t fear unexpected questions.**  
Sometimes, interviewers test how you **think under uncertainty** rather than what you already know. Take a pause, breathe, and answer calmly.
