# 💼 Interview Experience – Optum TDP Intern

**Type:** On-Campus Opportunity  
**Role:** Technology Development Program (Intern)  
**Total Rounds:** 2 (HR + Technical)

---

## 🗣️ Round 1 – HR Interview

The first round was a fairly **standard HR interview**.  
The interviewer asked a few **behavioral questions** and also inquired about my **extra-curricular activities** in college.

Some example questions were:

- “Tell me about your experiences outside academics.”
- “What kind of challenges have you faced while balancing academics and other work?”
- “How do you manage your time when working in teams?”

The round primarily tested **communication**, **confidence**, and **team spirit**.  
The tone was conversational and relaxed.

✅ **Outcome:** Cleared HR Round

---

## ⚙️ Round 2 – Technical Interview

The second round was a **technical interview** lasting around 40–45 minutes.  
The interviewer began by asking which **technology** or **part of my tech stack** I was most confident about.

When I mentioned **Machine Learning (ML)**, all further questions were based on ML concepts and my related projects.

Key discussion points included:

- Detailed explanation of my **ML project** and its implementation.
- Writing code for a **data cleaning function** that replaces invalid entries in a dataset column with zero.  
  📘 Similar Problem: [Data Cleaning – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/data-analysis/data-cleansing-introduction/)
- Explaining my **learning approach** -> how I explore new tools, technologies, and projects.
- How I keep myself **updated with the latest developments** in technology and AI trends.

Interestingly, I wasn’t asked a single **DSA question** in this round. The focus was entirely on **concept clarity**, **problem-solving attitude**, and **depth of project understanding**.

🧠 Helpful Prep:

- [Machine Learning Interview Questions – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/machine-learning-interview-questions/)
- [Python Data Preprocessing – GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/data-analysis/data-preprocessing-machine-learning-python/)

✅ **Outcome:** Selected for Optum TDP Intern

---

## 🌟 Overall Experience

The overall experience was **smooth, insightful, and surprisingly practical**.  
Unlike many other interviews, the focus wasn’t on trick questions or rote theory, it was about **understanding my thinking process** and **how I applied what I learned**.

Both interviewers were **friendly and encouraging**, which made the entire process less stressful and more of a learning conversation.  
The ML-related discussions gave me an opportunity to showcase both **technical confidence** and **passion for learning**.

---

## 💡 Advice for Others

🧩 **Master at least two things.**  
If you’re just starting out, **Web Development + Machine Learning** is the safest and most versatile combination.  
Most companies look for **full-stack candidates** who also have some exposure to **data and AI**.

⚙️ **Never stop your DSA grind.**  
But be smart about it — understand what level of DSA questions a company typically asks, and prepare accordingly instead of trying to cover everything.

💬 **Be curious.**  
Interviewers love candidates who show genuine curiosity about tech trends, especially in ML, data science, or product innovation.
